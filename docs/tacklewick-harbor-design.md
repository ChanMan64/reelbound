# Tacklewick Harbor — Level 1 Design

Tacklewick Harbor is an onboarding voyage, not a tutorial hallway. Each section introduces one mechanic in a forgiving context, rewards optional mastery with pearls or height, and then asks the player to reuse that mechanic under mild pressure.

## Teaching sequence

1. **Find Your Sea Legs — movement and variable jump**
   - A continuous dock prevents early deaths.
   - Low steps demonstrate tap-versus-hold jump height.
   - Pearls trace the intended arcs without being mandatory.

2. **Duck the Rigging — crouch and slide**
   - A short beam is exactly one strong slide long.
   - The floor remains continuous, so releasing speed only turns the attempt into a crouch rather than a fall.

3. **A Second Wind — double jump**
   - Pearls rise above solid ground.
   - Missing the second jump drops Finn safely onto the main dock.
   - The upper pearl route teaches mastery; the ground route preserves progress.

4. **Clear the Water — dash**
   - A wide, visually obvious water gap asks for jump-then-dash.
   - Double jump remains available as recovery.
   - A high optional platform lets confident players carry momentum upward.

5. **Wall Work — wall slide and wall jump**
   - A reachable left ledge feeds into a narrow shaft.
   - Zigzag pearls show the alternating wall-jump rhythm.
   - A solid floor catches every failed attempt.

6. **Old Reliable — reel and grapple**
   - The ordinary lower jump remains valid.
   - Gold rings provide a faster upper route and point toward the Ember Minnow.
   - The lure is a reward for exploration, not a key required to finish the voyage.

7. **Low and Loud — slide combat**
   - A low beam forces the same slide learned earlier.
   - Clackett waits immediately beyond it on flat ground.
   - Maintaining speed naturally launches the enemy; slowing down creates a readable normal encounter.

8. **Skipper Gull — attack reading**
   - Open air keeps the raised-wing dive telegraph visible.
   - Platforms on both sides give the player multiple evasive answers.
   - The water gap discourages blindly running through the encounter.

9. **Ride the Tide — moving platforms**
   - The first dock moves vertically from a safe departure point.
   - The second travels horizontally across a longer gap.
   - Pearl placement communicates timing and the intended landing side.

10. **The Harbor Test — final remix**
    - No new instructions appear.
    - Short gaps, a climbable wall, a grapple ring, Clackett, and Skipper can be approached with different combinations of movement.
    - The final dock is wide and calm so catching the Copper Koi feels like release after the test.

## Placement rules

- Main progression never requires a lure.
- New movement is practiced safely before it appears near an enemy or death pit.
- Checkpoints sit before dash/wall movement, combat, moving docks, and the final remix.
- Pearls communicate motion: arcs for jumps, straight lines for slides/dashes, zigzags for wall jumps, and timing points for moving docks.
- Enemies appear only after reel and movement attacks have been introduced.
